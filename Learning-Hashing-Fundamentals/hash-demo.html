Hashing Steps Overview

  3. Apply modulo 256 to keep the value within 0-255.
  4. Convert the final number to hexadecimal.
  5. Observe the avalanche effect: even a small change drastically changes the hash!

Why SHA-256 is Much Stronger

Our demo uses a simple hash function for learning. Real hash functions like SHA-256 are far more complex and secure.

  • Length and Output: Produces a 256-bit (32-byte) hash.
  • Complex Operations: Uses bitwise operations, modular additions, and logical functions on 512-bit input blocks.
  • Compression Function: Input is split, expanded, and combined through multiple rounds.
  • Avalanche Effect: A single bit change flips roughly half of the output bits unpredictably.
  • Security: Resistant to collisions and attacks, ideal for blockchain and digital signatures.

SHA-256 is used in Bitcoin and many secure systems. The core idea is the same as our demo: transforming input into a fixed-length fingerprint.

For those who want to explore SHA-256 in full detail, check out sha256algorithm.com.
